### The Systematic Plunder of Europe

When Adolf Hitler launched his war machine across Europe, the Nazi regime faced a major problem: **they were functionally bankrupt.** 

The German mark was a heavily manipulated, non-convertible currency that no foreign nation would accept for payment. To purchase the critical raw materials required to sustain a global war—such as tungsten from Portugal, iron ore from Sweden, and oil from Romania—the Nazis needed international "hard" currency. 

They needed gold.

To acquire it, the Nazi regime turned conquest into a massive, organized wealth-extraction operation. As the Wehrmacht rolled into foreign capitals, specialized squads immediately targeted the central bank vaults of occupied nations:
*   **Austria and Czechoslovakia:** Their entire national gold reserves were seized immediately upon annexation.
*   **Belgium:** Over 200 tons of Belgian gold, which had been sent to France for safekeeping, was betrayed by the Vichy regime and handed directly to the Reichsbank.
*   **The Netherlands:** Nearly 150 tons of Dutch gold was plundered from Amsterdam vaults.

In total, the Nazi regime looted an estimated **$600 million in sovereign gold** (equivalent to over $9 billion today) from the central banks of occupied Europe.

### The "Melmer" Gold: The Ultimate Tragedy

But the looting did not stop at central banks. 

Under the direction of SS leader Heinrich Himmler and Reichsbank governor Walther Funk, a highly secretive operation codenamed the **"Melmer Project"** was established. 

A specialized SS officer named Bruno Melmer was tasked with collecting personal valuables seized from victims of the Holocaust. Backpacks, suitcases, and crates filled with gold wedding bands, watches, jewelry, spectacle frames, and even gold dental fillings were shipped directly to the Reichsbank in Berlin.

There, the blood gold was melted down, refined into standard gold bars, and stamped with official Reichsbank assay marks. To disguise its horrific origins, much of this gold was deliberately backdated with pre-war minting dates (such as 1936 or 1937) before being pushed into international trade.

### The Swiss Laundering Connection

Because the Allies actively monitored and blacklisted German-looted gold, the Nazis could not spend stolen bullion directly on the global market. They needed a laundromat. 

**They found it in neutral Switzerland.**

Throughout the war, the Swiss National Bank (SNB) and several private Swiss commercial banks accepted hundreds of millions of dollars in Reichsbank gold. The Swiss bankers exchanged this looted gold for Swiss Francs—a highly respected, fully convertible neutral currency. 

The Nazi regime then used these Swiss Francs to purchase ball bearings, precision machinery, and raw materials from other neutral countries, effectively keeping their war machine fueled for years longer than would have otherwise been economically possible. 

Despite repeated warnings from the Allied powers, the laundering continued until the closing weeks of the war.

### The Legend of the Lost Gold Train

As the Reich crumbled in 1945, panic set in among Nazi leadership. They scrambled to hide their remaining gold reserves to keep them out of Allied and Soviet hands, burying them in mines, sinking them in alpine lakes, and hiding them in secret mountain vaults.

This panic birthed one of the greatest treasure legends of the 20th century: **The Nazi Gold Train.**

According to local lore, in late 1945, an armored train loaded with up to 300 tons of gold, jewels, and industrial masterpieces left the city of Breslau (now Wroclaw, Poland). The train allegedly entered a system of secret, unfinished underground tunnels blasted into the Owl Mountains near Walbrzych, and was sealed shut as the Red Army closed in.

Despite dozens of military searches, academic expeditions, and private treasure hunts using advanced ground-penetrating radar over the last 80 years, the "Ghost Train of Walbrzych" has never been found. It remains buried in the shadows of history.
